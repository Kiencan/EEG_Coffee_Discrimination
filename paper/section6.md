## 6. Conclusion

This paper investigated whether the aroma of *Coffea arabica* and *Coffea canephora* can be distinguished from single trial EEG under a strict subject independent evaluation. Using sixteen channel recordings at 100 Hz from twenty volunteers, a reproducible processing pipeline, and a transparent data quality audit that excluded eleven recordings affected by amplifier saturation, we benchmarked three representative machine learning models on spectral band power features and on a broader sweep of feature families. The best random forest reached an accuracy of 0.546 with an area under the ROC curve of 0.553, and no configuration across more than two hundred feature and model combinations exceeded a balanced accuracy of 0.55. A subject stratified permutation test confirmed that these scores are not statistically distinguishable from chance, a conclusion that also held for the coffee versus air contrast and for the prediction of subjective valence, intensity, and preference ratings.

We interpret these findings as an honest negative result that nevertheless contributes in three concrete ways. First, the released pipeline provides a reusable methodological template, including unit tested data loading, quality auditing, preprocessing, feature extraction, and LOSO evaluation, that other groups can apply to their own olfactory EEG datasets. Second, the sensitivity analysis on the full eighteen subject pool documents how hardware clipping can inflate cross subject accuracy by introducing a per subject confound, and it offers an empirical illustration of why a quality audit should be a non optional first stage in any EEG classification study. Third, the systematic mapping of feature families and classifiers, all converging to a chance level ceiling under LOSO evaluation, establishes a reference point against which more ambitious approaches can now be compared. Future work along the lines outlined in Section 5, in particular within subject modelling, sniff aligned event related features, functional connectivity with Riemannian alignment [21], and end to end deep architectures such as EEGNet [15] coupled with synchronised respiration recording, holds the most promise for closing the gap between within subject feasibility and subject independent generalisation in the EEG decoding of coffee aroma.
